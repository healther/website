Andreas Baumbach

I'm currently a PhD student at the Kirchhoff Institute for Physics at the University Heidelberg investigating statistical properties and peculiarities of spiking neural networks. As part of the Electronic Vision(s) Group I also participate in the deployment of the BrainScaleS neural network emulation system and in particular deal with the provisioning of the software dependency stack using Spack.

Over the years I contributed to a number of open source projects like

  • https://github.com/astropy/astropy
  • https://github.com/NeuralEnsemble/PyNN
  • https://github.com/spack/spack
  • https://github.com/numpy/numpy
most of which I ran across as part of my studies and most of which are really minor bug fixes, documentation or unit tests. The one example there might be Spack, where I'm actually a regular contributor.

As part of my PhD project I wrote an simulation generation, submission and collection tool experiment. It is a rather naive implementation and likely not particularly well optimized. Nevertheless it serves me well in generating large scale parameter sweeps, controlling the analysis of multi-terrabyte raw data and generating summaries of those.

In my free time (which sadly reduced significantly from my study days) I tend to start time consuming, but little-value projects like setting up a mail and web server or learning about the inner workings of the Linux kernel.

Finally there are these relaxing things like rock climbing and diving which I tend to do far too irregularly, but continuously tell me that I really ought to do more often.
